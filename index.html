Groud Transportation on Oahu

If you plan to stay on Oahu for more than just our wedding, we recommend renting a car from the airport.

If you are planning to mostly stay in Waikiki, but leave once or twice to explore, consider looking into renting a car from Hui. Similar to Zipcar, you can do short term rentals from Waikiki. Note that the company uses dash cams to moniter driving safety.

Note about rental cars: if you're from the mainland, when you fill gas you might notice that the splash guard and locking lever are missing from the nozzles. This is normal -just manually keep the lever depressed and it will still automatically shut off when your tank is full.

Hotel in Hawaii

The wedding will be held at the Hilton Hawaiian Village in Waikiki. You can use the coupon code WED to get a discount on rooms (Hawaii residents, contact Sheri for the Kama`aina code), but note that there may be better deals available, especially if you book early. This hotel will be ideal for guests who want a convenient location in relation to the wedding and want to stay in the heart of Waikiki with easy access to the beach and other tourist activities. The wedding venue is connected to the Kalia Tower, but the entire complex is easily accessible from our ceremony and reception area.

For another option, we highly recommend the Ala Moana Hotel. Located less than a mile from our wedding venue and attached to a large outdoor mall with many food options, this hotel is ideal for guests who want a cheaper but still convenient option. This location is walkable to our venue if you tolerate heat and humidity well (Hawaii is warm, even at night), and we recommend walking in groups at night.

We recommend supporting the local economy and NOT going with an AirBnB-type rental which are primarily owned by people who live out of state.

Note: if you plan on preparing your own food while in Hawaii, be sure to thoroughly wash all locally grown produce that you plan to consume raw. Thoroughly scrub every surface under running water.

Here are some of our favorite places on Oahu.

  • Places to Eat
    1. Zippy's Restaurant
      This is a popular local diner. Sheri's favorite meal is the chili and chicken mixed plate, but other popular dishes include their Zip Min, Zip Pac (President Obama's favorite), and Korean fried chicken. For dessert, try their Napples!
    2. Liliha Bakery
      They are famous for their coco puffs and local style breakfasts. Sheri also really enjoys their chantilly cakes - the frosting is unique and unmatched.
    3. Ala Moana Shopping Center
      This is a mall with a multitude of places to eat. For fast casual local flavors, check out the The Lanai.
    4. Haleiwa Joes
    5. 53 by the Sea
    6. Yohei Sushi
    7. Waiahole Poi Factory
    8. Leonard's Bakery
    9. Waiola Shave Ice
    10. Matsumoto's Shave Ice
    11. Foodland's Poke
    12. Times Supermarket
    13. Wholesale Unlimited
    14. Big Island Candies
  • Places to Learn
    1. Polynesian Cultural Center
      If you want to attend their luau dinner, be sure to reserve your space early!
      Come learn about history and culture throughout the Pacific at this world-class interactive cultural museum. Attend live cultural presentations, sample food, make traditional crafts, and much more. If you want to fully experience all of the villages, plan to be here for more than one day. It is about an hour drive from our wedding venue, so plan accordingly. There are also available shuttles from Waikiki. You will spend a lot of time outdoors, so wear sunscreen and consider bringing a hat/sunglasses.
    2. Bishop Museum
      Learn about the history of Hawaii, in a building constructed in part by Andrew's ancestors! And stop by the other buildings for some fun science exhibits.
    3. Plantation Village
      Learn how many early immigrants to Hawaii lived and worked, including Sheri's ancestors. This is partially outdoors, so wear sunscreen (and bug spray...get plenty mosquitoes).
    4. Pearl Harbor
      Pearl Harbor Aviation Museum
      Learn about Hawaii's history during WW2. It's worth it to go to the top of the tower at the aviation museum. Also be sure to check out the actual bullet holes in Hangar 49 from the attack on Pearl Harbor.
      While you're here, also stop by the USS Missouri and
    5. Iolani Palace
      Learn about Hawaiian history and culture in an actual royal palace.
    6. Honolulu Zoo and Waikiki Aquarium
      Be sure to say hi to Rusty the orangutan, and make a quick stop by the aquarium while you're in the area.
    7. Dole Plantation
